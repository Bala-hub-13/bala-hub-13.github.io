August-2025

Morning

Days began with an alarm. Stretched a bit to get myself moving and not to feel like a “folded lawn chair” at the sight of the bed. I drank some water, felt it sloshing in my empty stomach.
Toothbrush in hand, I peeked out my doorway like a detective. Who was that? Huh, you tree. Morning!
Safe, clear to proceed. On my left was the staircase leading up.
I sat on the chair and brushed my teeth. The time read '5:45'.
Afterward I went downstairs, splashed some water on my eyes, grabbed my laptop, and climbed back up, like I never left.

Started reading Derrida’s work (secondary source; don't judge). And the usual avian opera began. Cuckoos, squeals; maybe squirrels auditioning? Before all this was the rooster's morning cliche. Confirming the legends!

Here comes the red orb, but now it is yellowish orange. It painted the horizon, oddly dimming as the glare faded though. It grazed my right eye, not sliding smoothly but stuttering forward, like the whole star was moving in quantized steps.
Not impressed.

What I did

Pastas at other times. Made all kinds of milkshakes I liked.
Cooking is fun, preparatory but fun. I've to stop myself from eating half of it before it reaches the table.
And the peace of drinking ginger tea with the looming sunset; with all its saturated glory.
