🧬 Gene Retention is Evolutionary Laziness

Some genes aren’t kept because they’re essential. They're just too lazy to mutate out. Evolution isn’t a perfectionist, it’s a hoarder with boundary issues.

🧠 Opinions Are Cheap, Doubt is Expensive

We treat opinions like currency, but rarely do people invest in doubt. It costs nothing to say “I believe X.” It costs time, pain, and evidence to say “I used to believe X, and here’s why I changed.”

📉 Productivity is a Marketing Scheme

True productivity doesn't ask for your soul in exchange for a to-do list. Systems that don’t make room for boredom are just disguised burnout generators.

🪞 Why Your Profile Isn’t You

You're not your LinkedIn headline, your publication list, or even your bio. You’re the weird crossover of all the drafts you delete. That’s why notes matter—they’re half-formed and honest.

Science is Not a Product

Good science resists commodification. Its value lies in exploration, not just results. In a world optimized for productivity, thinking slowly is a quiet rebellion.
