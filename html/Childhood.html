On Childhood2025 · 09

This is going to be long, and this would grow up in time. So read at your leisure...

Childhood, rarely do we see the innocence of childhood as a gift, that feeling of the worst fear in your life was 'failing a subject', the stakes pretty high, back then. At least, in my life.
No, I'm not feeling nostalgic or melodramatic that it was gone.
But the realisation that how small that world was, only your school, neighbourhood, and the occasional trips. Yet how filled in that world was!
Banters, pranks, tournaments, and exams. You hate up showing on Monday, but only until you see your friend, then it would be all fun. I wonder that's how everybody felt! There were incidents that supports this, we would message others, letting them know in advance of our absence. This results in a particular ring of friends being absent.
Jealously was rarely visible, factions made up for diversity, most offensive thing was foul language.

I would wait eagerly for the evening Basketball practise, wondering at how could someone (coach) easily take a three pointer, I remember the time when he would be conversing with us and just a glance towards the board and 'whoosh' the sound of his shooting action so soft, it feels like a breeze on a sunny yet partly cloudy day.
And the thing that was my addiction back then, the sound of the ball going through the net without touching the back-board or the rim of the net.
That was an audio-visual odyssey. And only some feel and enjoy it. I call it the "Ring shot".

That is when we learnt to be different in our trigger movement, lay-up or lay-in, jump shot.
These would become our identity, our special move. All of us trying different things at our drill session.
Fridays would be a full 1-hour jog. Saturday is what everyone looks forward; game day.
I have this memory of a match in my fifth or sixth grade, the referee was my P.T, I got a pass near the half-court and I dribbled down to our attacking post, I told him that I would convert this shot, It was a lay-up, I always had a sweet spot for it; even though I rarely ever use it later at my life.
There is something so essentially different from lay-up and lay-in.
It's a whole different feel when you go for a lay-up; one shows the ball in his palm to the post, almost like an offer of the kindest intention. You don't make any noise, neither does the board.
Yep, I made that shot!
