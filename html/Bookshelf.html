2024 in Books

Fantasy. It was a genre that I loathed when I started reading seriously during my undergrad, I was more into non-fiction and popular science.

The shift into fantasy started small. I got hooked on Dune, so much that I reread it many times. At first, I thought I liked it just for the story, but over time I realized it was the layers; the allegory to oil, the prescient writing, the philosophy and the prophetic beliefs.

Then came The Witcher.
I read The Witcher, purely because there was a show on it (Why else?). That's why I read Dune, too.
Witcher is one of the best series I ever read, I’d sit down after lunch, lose myself in Sapkowski’s world and wonder how the clock had jumped to 4 PM. The prose felt lyrical though I know some call it dry. It's definitely one of the best, I would reread in the future.

By the end of the year, I had read The Witcher and the Harry Potter series. Somehow, the genre I once dismissed ended up being the one that gave me the most joy.

This was also the first time I read a Stephen King novel, Desperation. It would be an understatement to say that I loved it. The way the story is structured makes you feel safe about a character’s survival and then, suddenly, it happens. I didn’t expect it, but the story keeps moving relentlessly fast.

Satisfying character arcs? Tick. A gripping story? Tick. But most of all, the writing flows.
King’s prose felt almost lyrical, without ever dragging. I realized that I enjoyed his rhythm more than I thought I would, and it made me curious to explore more of his classics.

Here's to more such discoveries in the coming years.
