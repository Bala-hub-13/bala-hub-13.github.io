2024 in Books

Fantasy. It was a genre that I loathed when I started reading seriously during my undergrad, I was more into non-fiction and popular science.

Dune pulled me through first. At the surface, it was a story about power and prophecy, but each time I reread it, I found other things interesting: oil politics, ecological collapse, even the strange condition of belief itself.

Then came The Witcher.
I read The Witcher, purely because there was a show on it (Why else?). That's why I read Dune, too.
Witcher is a grimdark, almost philosophical series. (The Lady of the Lake is my favourite.) I’d sit down after lunch, lose myself in Sapkowski’s world and wonder how the clock had jumped to 4 PM. The prose felt lyrical though I know some call it dry. I would love to reread it in the future.

By the end of the year, I had read The Witcher and the Harry Potter series. Somehow, the genre I once dismissed ended up being the one that gave me the most joy.

This was also the first time I read a Stephen King novel, Desperation. It would be an understatement to say that I loved it. His sentences carried a music I hadn’t expected, where narrative rhythm mattered as much as terror.

Here's to more such discoveries in the coming years.
