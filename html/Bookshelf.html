2024 in Books

Fantasy. It was a genre that I loathed when I started reading seriously during my undergrad, I was more into non-fiction and popular science.

Dune pulled me through first. At the surface, it was a story about power and prophecy, but there was this nagging feeling to read it again. Took me some time to find that it was the writing! Herbert doesn't keep anything mysterious; he reveals all of it, multiple POV's in a single page.

Then came The Witcher.
I read The Witcher, purely because there was a show on it (Why else?). That's why I read Dune, too.
Witcher is a grimdark series. (The Lady of the Lake is my favourite.) I’d sit down after lunch, lose myself in Sapkowski’s world and wonder how the clock had jumped to 4 PM. The prose felt lyrical though I know some call it dry. I would love to reread it in the future.

By the end of the year, I had read the Harry Potter series too.

Here's to more such discoveries in the coming years.
